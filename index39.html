The Digital Ink
Revolution

Beyond the blinding glow of OLED screens and high-refresh-rate LCD monitors lies the realm of electrophoretic display arrays. Here, physical microcapsules filled with positively charged white particles and negatively charged black particles are mechanically pushed to the surface.

The result is a screen that does not emit light. It merely reflects ambient photons, providing an optical experience utterly indistinguishable from a printed page pressed in the year 1890.

This layout operates under a strict two-color constraint. Gradients are entirely forbidden. Shading is accomplished via algorithmic dithering matrices. The interaction paradigm demands that smooth transitions be purged, replaced instead with binary, immediate state changes.
